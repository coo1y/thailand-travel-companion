Weather Forecast

**Bangkok (July):** - **Avg. temp:** 26–33 °C (78–91 °F) - **Conditions:** Frequent heavy afternoon showers, overcast to mostly cloudy (cloud cover >90% most days), high humidity - **Tip:** Pack a lightweight rain jacket or compact umbrella and quick-drying clothes; schedule temple visits and outdoor markets in the morning to avoid typical afternoon downpours. Expect steamy, humid heat and brief but drenching thunderstorms that rarely last more than an hour. Indoor attractions and rooftop pools are ideal for midday breaks.

Place Options

### Temples (Wats): - **Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn):** A must-see riverside temple famed for its porcelain mosaic spires that gleam at sunrise and sunset; open daily from 8:00–18:00. - **Wat Pho (Temple of the Reclining Buddha):** See the enormous gold Reclining Buddha and enjoy skilled Thai massage at this historic site, open daily 8:00–18:30. - **Wat Phra Kaew & The Grand Palace:** Bangkok’s top spiritual attraction, the Grand Palace and Emerald Buddha dazzle every day from 8:30–15:30 (dress code enforced). - **Wat Traimit (Temple of the Golden Buddha):** A smaller gem near Chinatown, home to the world's largest solid gold Buddha, open daily 8:00–17:00. - **Hidden Gem – Wat Ratchanatdaram (Loha Prasat):** The “metal castle” offers stunning city views; few tourists venture up its spiral staircases. Open daily 8:00–17:00. ### Shopping Malls: - **Siam Paragon:** Bangkok’s luxury shopping mecca with designer boutiques, SEA LIFE aquarium, and world-class food court; daily 10:00–22:00. - **CentralWorld:** One of Southeast Asia’s largest malls, featuring global brands, events, a gourmet food hall, and open-air beer garden (seasonal); daily 10:00–22:00. - **MBK Center:** Famed for budget shopping, electronics, and souvenirs—lively, quirky, and a real slice of local life; daily 10:00–22:00. - **IconSiam:** Riverside luxury mall with spectacular river views, a floating market, Thailand-themed shops, and hundreds of restaurants; daily 10:00–22:00. - **Terminal 21:** Themed like an airport, each floor represents a global destination; Pier 21 food court is a favorite among locals and budget travelers; daily 10:00–22:00. ### Authentic Thai Food & Hidden Foodie Gems: - **Chinatown (Yaowarat Road):** Bangkok’s street food capital, best at night (17:00–midnight) with legendary stalls for roast duck, oyster omelet, and mango sticky rice. - **Wang Lang Market:** A favorite of Thais, this riverside market (Mon–Fri 10:00–17:00, Sat–Sun 08:00–17:00) is packed with snacks, curries, and legendary fried chicken. - **Ruam Sab Market (Weekdays Only):** Hidden food market for local office workers with fresh, cheap lunchtime eats. Weekdays, especially vibrant 11:00–14:00. - **Terminal 21’s Pier 21 Food Court:** Possibly the city’s best-value food court, offering authentic som tum, noodles, desserts from 10:00–22:00 daily. - **Gourmet Market @ Siam Paragon:** Premium food court experience with everything from pad Thai to international treats; open 10:00–22:00. - **Bangkok Food Tours:** Book an evening or morning food tour for hidden markets, boat noodles, and savory snacks in local neighborhoods; daily departures, bookings essential. - **Silom Night Market:** After dark (17:00–midnight), Silom’s side streets brim with spicy curries and unique nighttime snacks rarely found elsewhere. ### Markets, Events & Unique Experiences: - **Chatuchak Weekend Market:** The world’s largest weekend market—over 15,000 stalls—for fashion, gadgets, antiques, and endless Thai food; Sat–Sun 09:00–18:00. - **Asiatique The Riverfront:** Open daily 16:00–00:00, this waterside night market fuses open-air shopping with river views and bustling bars. - **Damnoen Saduak Floating Market (Day Trip):** Mornings only, best before 11:00; paddle through canals and sample noodles and coconut snacks direct from the boat. - **Erawan Museum:** Known for its giant three-headed elephant and surreal sculptures; daily 9:00–19:00, ideal for those seeking Thai mythology and artistry. - **MOCA Bangkok (Modern Art):** A contemporary art haven open Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00, perfect for rainy afternoons. - **ChangChui Creative Park:** A sprawling art/theater/market/exhibition space open 11:00–23:00 (closed Mondays)—come for street art, night bites, and cool cafés. - **Airplane Graveyard:** Explore decommissioned jumbo jets transformed into an urban curiosity; Travel Tips

- Carry small change for street food and transport. - Dress politely for temples (shoulders/knees covered). - Use Grab/official taxis (by meter, not fixed fares) for safety. - Lightweight umbrella and quick-dry clothes are must-haves for July’s daily thunderstorms. - Morning visits beat crowds and heat at temples and outdoor markets. - Tap water is not drinkable—buy bottled or ask for "น้ำเปล่า" (nam plao).
